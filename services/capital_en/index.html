CAPITAL - Value as the outcome of decisions over time


CAPITAL is the level at which the decisions of the administrative body produce value over time.
Value is formed as the cumulative result of choices that affect continuity, sustainability, resource allocation, and the company's ability to withstand complexity and discontinuity.


It reflects the way in which risk is assumed, governed, and integrated into strategic and operational decisions. In this context, the company’s ability to measure the effects of decisions through relevant, consistent, and usable information in the decision-making process is important.

Value is consolidated when decisions are based on adequate information that is consistent with the company’s structure. Responsibility is measured by the ability to reconstruct the link between decisions made, use of available information, and maintaining balance over time.
CAPITAL expresses the company’s ability to sustain growth, investments, and extraordinary operations on a solid decision-making basis. In this context.

LGMC supports directors in setting up structures that can support value, continuity, and reliability over time.
